About me

I am a tenure-track Assistant Professor at Lawrence Technological University, where I teach courses in robotics, control systems, and artificial intelligence and lead research on intelligent and interactive robotic systems. My work focuses on human–robot interaction and learning-based decision-making, with an emphasis on trust and user response to robotic behaviors in collaborative environments.

I received my Ph.D. from The University of Alabama under the supervision of Prof. Nader Jalili, where my doctoral research focused on human-in-the-loop control, trust modeling in human–robot collaboration, and brain–computer interfaces.

🧠 Research Interests

My research studies how robots can make adaptive decisions in collaboration with humans by modeling trust, user response, and task context. I develop interactive robotic frameworks that integrate sensing, signal processing, and machine learning, and evaluate them through human-subject experimentation.

🧪 Research Themes and Methods

  • Trust and user-state modeling: EEG/pupillometry-informed estimation of user response and team dynamics
  • Learning-based decision-making: adaptive autonomy policies for collaborative tasks
  • Interactive system development: sensing, real-time processing, and human-in-the-loop evaluation
