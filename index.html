Bio

I am a tenure-track Assistant Professor at Lawrence Technological University, where I teach courses in robotics, control systems, and artificial intelligence and lead research on intelligent and interactive robotic systems. My work focuses on human–robot interaction and learning-based decision-making, with an emphasis on understanding trust and human responses to robotic behaviors in collaborative environments.

My research integrates robotics system development, sensing, signal processing, and artificial intelligence to design adaptive robotic frameworks that can respond to human state and behavior. By combining machine learning with human-subject experimentation, I investigate how robotic systems can make more effective and trustworthy decisions when operating alongside humans.

I received my Ph.D. from The University of Alabama under the supervision of Prof. Nader Jalili, where my doctoral research focused on human-in-the-loop control, trust modeling in human–robot collaboration, and brain–computer interfaces.

Research Areas

Human state modeling for adaptive autonomy
EEG/pupillometry-based inference of trust, engagement, and presence for closed-loop adaptation.
Perception & mapping for micro-UAS
VIO/SLAM, point clouds, TSDF/ESDF mapping, and ROS 2 integration for real-time autonomy.
Digital twins for HRI and manufacturing
Immersive interfaces and monitoring to connect physical systems and virtual environments.
