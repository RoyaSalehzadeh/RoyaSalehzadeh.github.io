🏭 Workforce Development & Education

Development of IMaDE’s Education and Workforce Training Structure

University of Alabama • IMaDE Center Industry 4.0 Digital Twins Robotics & Automation

Contributed to building the instructional and workforce-development infrastructure for the B.S. in Manufacturing Systems Engineering. The initiative aligned the curriculum with Industry 4.0 priorities including automation, robotics, PLCs, digital twins, smart manufacturing, and data-driven engineering.

  • Developed 15 modular courses, lab manuals, and digital-twin-enabled instructional materials.
  • Implemented experiential lab workflows connecting automation hardware with virtual manufacturing environments.
  • Supported partnerships with Mercedes-Benz U.S. International, EBZ SysTec, Intelitek, Learning Labs, Rockwell Automation, and TransfrVR.

Smart Manufacturing Systems Teacher Training Workshop

Alabama IMaDE Center • Two-day workshop K–12 Educator Training Hands-on Labs

Organized and delivered a two-day Smart Manufacturing Systems workshop to support manufacturing education across Alabama. The program introduced educators to robotics, PLCs, smart sensors, VR, and automated systems through lectures, lab tours, and hands-on sessions.

  • Collaborators included Intelitek, Mercedes-Benz U.S. International, Alabama Robotics Technology Park, TransfrVR, and the Alabama Community College System.
  • Provided practical exposure and implementation resources for regional manufacturing programs.
